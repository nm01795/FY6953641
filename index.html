Abstract

  This paper presents compares liquid methane (LOX/CH4) and kerosene (LOX/RP-1) as rocket fuels for reusable booster engines. The study evaluates the performance, e.g specific impulse, density, combustion temp and environmental factors. Based on published studies, and compares the feasibility of using these fuels in reusable rockets


  
introduction

 The fuel that a rocket engine uses determines the efficiency, the cost and reusability of rockets. Firstly, Kerosene particularly RP-1, 
 has been the main fuel source in the past which has powered the most popular launch vehicles such as the Saturn V and Soyuz rockets. 
 Whereas Methane is now becoming a highly used fuel source in the space industry for the next generation of rockets especially reusable 
 rockets for example Space X’s starship and Blue Origin’s New Glenn, Where reusability is very important as a factor for modern rockets 
 due to the possibilities of flight to other planets such as mars and the possibility for synthesis of these fuels on extraterrestrial planets.
